About Kiwiü•ù

The kiwi fruit, a fuzzy brown oval, is a nutritional powerhouse packed with vitamin C, fiber, and antioxidants. Native to China, it's now cultivated worldwide, particularly in New Zealand, Italy, and Chile. Its sweet, tangy flesh, dotted with tiny black seeds, is enjoyed fresh, in smoothies, and even as a meat tenderizer. Beyond its delicious taste, the kiwi offers numerous health benefits, including boosting immunity, promoting digestion, and protecting cells from damage.
